THE WILD....

Sinharaja

One of Sri Lanka's truest gems , Sinharaja forest reservehas been recognized on a global scale by being officially considered a UNESCO world heritage site in the year 1988. Brimming with biodiversity Sinharaja forest reserve teams with life and greenery. The sheer size of the reserve makes it extend its reach over two of Sri lanka's provinces namely the sabaragamuw and southern provinces. SInharaja is a tropical rainforest which covers an area of 11000 Ha plays host to many rare tree types and a hefty 60 percent of those are endemic to Sri lanka.

yala srilanka

Located 260km to the southeast of colombo Yala occuies a ehopping 378 sq miles of land. Yala is home to many wildlife icluding the pathera pandas kotiya a species endemic to Sri lanka. yala is a wildlife sanctuary playing host to 44 species of mammals and 215 species of birds. Aside from leopards and elephants sloth bears ,sambars,jackals and peacocks along with mnay more species can be found there

Udawalawa

Udawalawa national park was named as the fifth national park of sri lanka in 1972. It is one of Sri lanka's number one tourist destinations. this is because of multiple elephants that roam the park. Udawlawa covers an area of 31000 hectares and has a expanansive cover of scrubland. In the close vicinity of the national park, a water reservoire covering a span of 3500 hecatares is located. In addition to elephants visitors get to see peacocks, deer and even leopards during their safari adventures
